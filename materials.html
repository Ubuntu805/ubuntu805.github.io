Materials in Thermal Engineering

Material selection is fundamental to thermal design. Conductivity, weight, cost, and manufacturability all play a role in choosing the right material for a cooling solution.

Common Thermal Materials

Material k (W/m·K) Density (g/cm³) Notes
Copper (C110) 390 8.9 Best conductor, heavy, expensive
Aluminum (6061) 167 2.7 Lightweight, easy to machine
Graphite (in-plane) 300–1500 1.5–2.2 Anisotropic, excellent spreader
AlSiC 170–200 3.0 CTE-matched to silicon
Diamond composite 500–2000 3.5 Exotic, ultra-high performance

Selection Criteria

  • Thermal conductivity (bulk and directional)
  • CTE match to adjacent components
  • Weight constraints (aerospace, mobile)
  • Machinability and manufacturing method
  • Corrosion resistance and surface finish
