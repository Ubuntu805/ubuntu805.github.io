Cold Plates: Design & Performance

Cold plates are one of the most effective liquid cooling solutions for high-heat-flux electronics. They use internal channels to route coolant directly beneath heat-generating components.

Types of Cold Plates

  • Tube-in-plate – copper or stainless tubes pressed into an aluminum plate. Simple, low cost.
  • Brazed fin – internal fin structures brazed between two plates. High performance, compact.
  • Micro-channel – very fine channels (< 1 mm). Excellent heat transfer but higher pressure drop.
  • Gun-drilled – deep-drilled passages in a solid block. Good for custom geometries.

Key Design Considerations

  • Flow rate vs. pressure drop tradeoff
  • Channel geometry and fin density
  • Material selection (Al vs. Cu vs. hybrid)
  • Mounting and TIM interface
  • Corrosion and coolant compatibility

Performance Metrics

Thermal resistance (°C/W) from junction to coolant is the key metric. Typical values range from 0.01–0.1 °C/W depending on size and design.
