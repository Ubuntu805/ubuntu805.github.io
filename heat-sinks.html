Heat Sinks: Fundamentals & Selection

Heat sinks dissipate thermal energy by increasing the surface area exposed to a cooling medium (usually air). Choosing the right heat sink depends on power, airflow, and space constraints.

Common Types

  • Extruded – low cost, moderate performance. Great for general-purpose cooling.
  • Bonded fin – fins bonded to a base plate. Higher fin density possible.
  • Skived – fins sliced from a single block. Excellent fin-to-base conductance.
  • Forged/Die-cast – for high-volume, custom geometries.
  • Vapor chamber base – spreads heat before reaching fins. Ideal for hot-spot mitigation.

Design Parameters

  • Fin height, thickness, and spacing
  • Base thickness and spreading resistance
  • Natural vs. forced convection
  • Ducted vs. un-ducted airflow
  • Surface treatments (anodizing, black coating for radiation)

Thermal Resistance

Total resistance = Rspreading + Rconduction + Rconvection. Optimizing each component yields the best overall performance.
